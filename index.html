FAQ

Pergola & Patio Contractor Questions

These FAQs are written for homeowners searching for a pergola builder, patio cover contractor, patio enclosure or outdoor kitchen in the Houston area.

Look for a pergola contractor or patio contractor who is licensed and insured, has photos of completed projects and understands Houston weather and HOA rules. Ask for a written estimate, a clear timeline and details on the materials and footings they plan to use so your pergola or patio cover holds up long term.
Pricing depends on size, design, materials and whether the structure is attached to your home or freestanding. A simple wood pergola will cost less than a solid-roof patio cover or a pergola extension tied into an outdoor kitchen. The best way to get accurate pricing is to schedule a free on-site estimate so we can measure your space and talk through options.
The best patio cover contractor for you is the one who understands your goals and builds a structure that fits your home. Pergola Builder Houston focuses specifically on custom pergolas, patio covers and outdoor kitchens. We match roof lines and finishes, use quality materials and provide licensed, insured installation throughout the Houston area.
A pergola is an open-roof structure with rafters that provide partial shade. A patio cover has a solid roof that keeps out sun and rain. A gazebo is a freestanding structure, often with a full roof and railings. An arbor is a smaller entry feature that frames a walkway or gate. We build wood pergolas, gazebos, arbors and solid patio covers so you can choose the right option for your yard.
In many cases we can extend an existing patio cover or wood pergola by adding new posts, beams and rafters that tie into the original structure. We will inspect the existing footings and framing first to make sure a pergola extension is safe. If your old structure was not built correctly, we may recommend replacing it instead of building on top of it.
Our main focus is pergolas, patio covers and open-air outdoor living spaces. However, we can sometimes design a patio enclosure or screened room that connects to a new or existing cover. During your consultation we can compare a traditional pergola, solid patio cover and enclosure so you can choose the right level of shade and protection.

Most of our projects are custom wood pergolas and patio covers that match the trim and fascia on your home. In some situations we can offer aluminum or composite options when low maintenance is a priority. We will walk you through the pros and cons of each material for Houston weather during your estimate.
Once permits and materials are ready, most pergola projects take only a few days of on-site work. More complex patio covers, gazebos, arbors and outdoor kitchen builds may take one to three weeks depending on size and design. We will give you a realistic schedule before we begin construction.
Some pergolas and many patio covers do require permits or HOA approval. As a pergola contractor we regularly work with local guidelines and can help with drawings, engineering details and paperwork so your project is approved before construction starts.
